Welcome to Agile Methodology

A flexible approach to software development that values collaboration, adaptability, and customer satisfaction.

What is Agile?

Agile is a set of principles for software development under which requirements and solutions evolve through the collaborative effort of self-organizing and cross-functional teams. Agile promotes flexible, rapid responses to change and the frequent delivery of working software.

Benefits of Agile

Increased Flexibility

Agile allows teams to quickly adapt to changes in requirements, technologies, and market conditions.

Improved Collaboration

Frequent communication and collaboration among team members and stakeholders ensure transparency and alignment.

Faster Delivery

With regular iterations and deliveries, Agile allows products to be delivered faster and more efficiently.

How Agile Works

Agile follows an iterative process, where work is divided into small cycles called "sprints". Each sprint focuses on a specific set of features or tasks that are completed within a short time frame, usually 1-4 weeks. Here’s how it works:

  • Planning: Teams define the scope of the sprint.
  • Development: Teams start building the software incrementally.
  • Review: At the end of the sprint, the team presents the work done and collects feedback.
  • Retrospective: The team reflects on the sprint to improve the next one.
